Simply Collaborate: Keeping IP Ownership in Collaborations Simple mdi-heart mdi-dots-vertical test1 The Problem with "Jointly Owned" IP

Joint collaboration is essential to innovation, particularly when enginuity and innovation is required to tackle problems such as COVID-19. In our experience, many organizations simply agree they will "jointly own" the work products. However, did you know there is significant ambiguity what "joint ownership" means under the laws of Canada and other jurisdictions? For example, do you always need the approval of the other person in order to use, sell, make available, distribute or market the work product? You might...

For organizations that do not have significant legal resource, we propose a very simple, 2-option approach:

  • Tell us if you're collaborating to develop an open source project or not.
  • We'll provide you with a Collaboration Agreement that is fair and equitable. One party is provided ownership rights, and the other party very broad, perpetual license rights that are almost tantamount to ownership. For non-open source projects where each person brings IP to the table, we'll clearly set out that each party will own the IP that it brings to the table.
  • We'll provide you with a simple charter document to remind you how this works.
  • We'll even automatically set up GitHUB Account, designed for collaboration and is specifically aligned with the Collaboration Agreements.

That's it!
